
# What is Clustering?

Clustering is an unsupervised machine learning technique used to group similar data points together.

Unsupervised means there are no labels or correct answers given.
The algorithm finds patterns on its own.

Simple Example

Imagine you have a basket of fruits mixed together:

🍎 , 🍌 , 🍊 

Without anyone telling you which fruit is which, you naturally separate them into groups based on their similarities.

* Group 1 → Apples
* Group 2 → Bananas
* Group 3 → Oranges

This process of making similar groups is called clustering.

---

# Real-Life Examples of Clustering

1. Customer Segmentation

A shopping website groups customers based on buying habits.

Example:

    Students
    Professionals
    Senior citizens

Then it shows different advertisements to each group.

2. Movie Recommendations

Streaming services group users who watch similar movies.

If people in your cluster liked a movie, it recommends it to you.

3. Organizing Photos

Your phone groups photos of the same person together without anyone labeling them.

---

# Why Do We Need Clustering?

Suppose you have 10,000 customers.

Looking at each customer individually is difficult.

Instead, clustering creates groups like:

Cluster A → Young students

Cluster B → Working professionals

Cluster C → Families

Now businesses can understand customers much more easily.
