# Vanishing Gradient Problem

Traditional RNNs suffer from the vanishing gradient problem, where gradients shrink exponentially as they are backpropagated through time. This makes it incredibly difficult for standard RNNs to remember information from many steps ago. LSTMs solve this by introducing a cell state and a series of gates that regulate the flow of information.

The gradient is essentially zero by the time it reaches the earliest words. As a result, the model cannot learn long-range dependencies, such as connecting a subject at the beginning of a sentence to a verb much later.

Why LSTM Solves This

An LSTM introduces a cell state that provides a more direct path for information and gradients. Instead of repeatedly multiplying by the recurrent weight and activation derivative at every step, the forget gate can preserve information by outputting values close to 1. This helps gradients remain much larger over long sequences, allowing the network to learn dependencies across dozens or even hundreds of time steps.
